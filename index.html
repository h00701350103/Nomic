I.R. 101

All players must always abide by all the rules then in effect, in the form in which they are then in effect. The rules in the Initial Set are in effect whenever a game begins. The Initial Set consists of Rules 101-116 (immutable) and 201-213 (mutable).

  • Part of the Initial Set
I.R. 102

Initially rules in the 100's are immutable and rules in the 200's are mutable. Rules subsequently enacted or transmuted (that is, changed from immutable to mutable or vice versa) may be immutable or mutable regardless of their numbers, and rules in the Initial Set may be transmuted regardless of their numbers.

  • Part of the Initial Set
I.R. 103

A rule-change is any of the following: (1) the enactment, repeal, or amendment of a mutable rule; (2) the enactment, repeal, or amendment of an amendment of a mutable rule; or (3) the transmutation of an immutable rule into a mutable rule or vice versa.

(Note: This definition implies that, at least initially, all new rules are mutable; immutable rules, as long as they are immutable, may not be amended or repealed; mutable rules, as long as they are mutable, may be amended or repealed; any rule of any status may be transmuted; no rule is absolutely immune to change.)

  • Part of the Initial Set
I.R. 104

All rule-changes proposed in the proper way shall be voted on. They will be adopted if and only if they receive the required number of votes.

  • Part of the Initial Set
I.R. 105

All players are eligible voters. Non-players are not eligible voters.

  • Part of the Initial Set

  • Amended by Proposal #0C
  • Transmuted into an immutable rule by Proposal #0C
  • Judgment 11:17-WML-003: “no other mechanism may govern eligibility until and unless rule changes dictate otherwise”
I.R. 106

All proposed rule-changes shall be written down before they are voted on. If they are adopted, they shall guide play in the form in which they were voted on.

  • Part of the Initial Set
I.R. 107

No rule-change may take effect earlier than the moment of the completion of the vote that adopted it, even if its wording explicitly states otherwise. No rule-change may have retroactive application.

  • Part of the Initial Set
I.R. 108

Each proposed rule-change shall be given a number for reference. The numbers shall begin with 301, and each rule-change proposed in the proper way shall receive the next successive integer, whether or not the proposal is adopted.

If a rule is repealed and reenacted, it receives the number of the proposal to reenact it. If a rule is amended or transmuted, it receives the number of the proposal to amend or transmute it. If an amendment is amended or repealed, the entire rule of which it is a part receives the number of the proposal to amend or repeal the amendment.

  • Part of the Initial Set
I.R. 110

In a conflict between a mutable and an immutable rule, the immutable rule takes precedence and the mutable rule shall be entirely void. For the purposes of this rule a proposal to transmute an immutable rule does not "conflict" with that immutable rule.

  • Part of the Initial Set
I.R. 111

If a rule-change as proposed is unclear, ambiguous, paradoxical, or destructive of play, or if it arguably consists of two or more rule-changes compounded or is an amendment that makes no difference, or if it is otherwise of questionable value, then the other players may suggest amendments or argue against the proposal before the vote. A reasonable time must be allowed for this debate. The proponent decides the final form in which the proposal is to be voted on and, unless the Judge has been asked to do so, also decides the time to end debate and vote.

  • Part of the Initial Set
  • Judgment 11:17-OV-006: “the ‘reasonable time’ clause trumps the proponent’s power to end debate”
I.R. 112

The state of affairs that constitutes winning may not be altered from achieving n points to any other state of affairs. The magnitude of n and the means of earning points may be changed, and rules that establish a winner when play cannot continue may be enacted and (while they are mutable) be amended or repealed.

  • Part of the Initial Set
I.R. 113

A player always has the option to forfeit the game rather than continue to play or incur a game penalty. No penalty worse than losing, in the judgment of the player to incur it, may be imposed.

  • Part of the Initial Set
I.R. 114

There must always be at least one mutable rule. The adoption of rule-changes must never become completely impermissible.

  • Part of the Initial Set
I.R. 115

Rule-changes that affect rules needed to allow or apply rule-changes are as permissible as other rule-changes. Even rule-changes that amend or repeal their own authority are permissible. No rule-change or type of move is impermissible solely on account of the self-reference or self-application of a rule.

  • Part of the Initial Set
I.R. 116

Whatever is not prohibited or regulated by a rule is permitted and unregulated, with the sole exception of changing the rules, which is permitted only when a rule or set of rules explicitly or implicitly permits it.

  • Part of the Initial Set
  • Judgment 11:17-WML-001: “actions regulated by rules are impliedly prohibited except for the operation of those rules”
M.R. 201

Players take turns in order according to a random ordered list generated by wilarseny using the Google Sheets "randomize range" function, and stored in the rules spreadsheet on the "score" tab. Turns may not be skipped or passed, and parts of turns may not be omitted, except by failure to submit a proposal. All players begin with zero points.

M.R. 202

One turn consists of two parts in this order: (1) proposing one rule-change and having it voted on, and (2) throwing one die once and adding the number of points on its face to one's score.

In mail and computer games, instead of throwing a die, players subtract 291 from the ordinal number of their proposal and multiply the result by the fraction of favorable votes it received, rounded to the nearest integer. (This yields a number between 0 and 10 for the first player, with the upper limit increasing by one each turn; more points are awarded for more popular proposals.)

  • Part of the Initial Set
  • Judgment 11:17-SV-004: votes made by non-eligible voters “shall be included in the count for the purpose of rule 202 yet not be considered to determine whether the proposal is accepted or not”
  • Amended by proposal #310, to become M.R. 310
M.R. 203

A rule-change is adopted if and only if the vote is unanimous among the eligible voters. If this rule is not amended by the end of the second complete circuit of turns, it automatically changes to require only a simple majority.

M.R. 207

M.R. 208

The winner is the first player to achieve 100 (positive) points.

In mail and computer games, the winner is the first player to achieve 200 (positive) points.

  • Part of the Initial Set
M.R. 209

At no time may there be more than 25 mutable rules.

  • Part of the Initial Set
M.R. 210

Players may not conspire or consult on the making of future rule-changes unless they are team-mates.

The first paragraph of this rule does not apply to games by mail or computer.

  • Part of the Initial Set
M.R. 211

If two or more mutable rules conflict with one another, or if two or more immutable rules conflict with one another, then the rule with the lowest ordinal number takes precedence.

If at least one of the rules in conflict explicitly says of itself that it defers to another rule (or type of rule) or takes precedence over another rule (or type of rule), then such provisions shall supersede the numerical method for determining precedence.

If two or more rules claim to take precedence over one another or to defer to one another, then the numerical method again governs.

  • Part of the Initial Set
  • Judgment 11:17-WML-001: “M.R. 211 only applies to mutable rules”
M.R. 212

If players disagree about the legality of a move or the interpretation or application of a rule, then the player preceding the one moving is to be the Judge and decide the question. Disagreement for the purposes of this rule may be created by the insistence of any player. This process is called invoking Judgment.

When Judgment has been invoked, the next player may not begin his or her turn without the consent of a majority of the other players.

The Judge's Judgment may be overruled only by a unanimous vote of the other players taken before the next turn is begun. If a Judge's Judgment is overruled, then the player preceding the Judge in the playing order becomes the new Judge for the question, and so on, except that no player is to be Judge during his or her own turn or during the turn of a team-mate.

Unless a Judge is overruled, one Judge settles all questions arising from the game until the next turn is begun, including questions as to his or her own legitimacy and jurisdiction as Judge.

New Judges are not bound by the decisions of old Judges. New Judges may, however, settle only those questions on which the players currently disagree and that affect the completion of the turn in which Judgment was invoked. All decisions by Judges shall be in accordance with all the rules then in effect; but when the rules are silent, inconsistent, or unclear on the point at issue, then the Judge shall consider game-custom and the spirit of the game before applying other standards.

  • Part of the Initial Set
  • Judgment 11:17-WML-001: “M.R. 212 encourages the use of legislative history as an aid for judicial decisionmaking”
  • Amended by proposal #304, to become M.R. 304
M.R. 213

If the rules are changed so that further play is impossible, or if the legality of a move cannot be determined with finality, or if by the Judge's best reasoning, not overruled, a move appears equally legal and illegal, then the first player unable to complete a turn is the winner.

This rule takes precedence over every other rule determining the winner.

  • Part of the Initial Set
M.R. 214

Votes must be cast by saying "yes" or "no" in the #voting channel. Votes take immediate effect once made. Editing or deleting your vote results in forfeited vote; you count for the purposes of "whether everyone has voted," but your vote does not count for "yes" or "no."

Voting ends when either a) a vote is conclusively determined, b) every eligible player has voted, or c) 12 hours have passed since the vote proposal, whichever comes first.

  • Created by proposal #0B
  • Judgment 11:17-WML-002: “Yes means yes”
M.R. 215

When a vote concludes, an admin must post the results in #rules-changelog, along with any corresponding changes to mutable or immutable rules. The next player by turn order must make a rule proposal by posting in #voting within 12 hours or their turn is forfeit.

M.R. 304

If players disagree about the legality of a move or the interpretation or application of a rule, then the player preceding the one moving is to be the Judge and decide the question. Disagreement for the purposes of this rule may be created by the insistence of any player, except the Judge. This process is called invoking Judgment.

The Judge's Judgment may be overruled only by a 4/5 majority vote of the other players. If a Judge's Judgment is overruled, then the player preceding the Judge in the playing order becomes the new Judge for the question, and so on, except that no player is to be Judge on the question on which they invoked Judgment.

Unless a Judge is overruled, one Judge settles all questions arising from the game until the next turn is begun, including questions as to their own legitimacy and jurisdiction as Judge.

New Judges are not bound by the decisions of old Judges. New Judges may, however, settle only those questions on which the players currently disagree and that affect the completion of the turn in which Judgment was invoked. All decisions by Judges shall be in accordance with all the rules then in effect; but when the rules are silent, inconsistent, or unclear on the point at issue, then the Judge shall consider game-custom and the spirit of the game before applying other standards.

M.R. 307

Players take turns in order according to a random ordered list generated by wilarseny using the Google Sheets "randomize range" function, and stored in the rules spreadsheet on the "score" tab. Should a player not be within the turn order, said player will be placed at the end of the turn order. The first new player to join will be placed in the slot after the last player in the order, the second player two slots after the last one, and so one.

M.R. 310

One turn consists of two parts in this order:

  1. proposing one bill and having it voted on, and
  2. adding the number of favorable votes it received to one's score.

A bill consists of one or more proposals using the bill as a proxy. A bill must pass the adoption requirements of all of its proposals in order to pass. If a bill is passed, all of its proposals go into effect in the order that they appear in the bill, otherwise none of them go into effect.

  • Proposal #310 passed by 2/3 majority vote of 13–1
  • Amends M.R. 202
  • Amended by proposal #315, to become M.R. 315
M.R. 311

Rule-changes that transmute immutable rules into mutable rules may be adopted if and only if the vote is unanimous among the eligible voters. Transmutation shall not be implied, but must be stated explicitly in a proposal to take effect.

M.R. 314

A bill consists of one or more proposed rule-changes, which use the bill as a proxy.

If a bill contains one or more proposed rule-changes that require a unanimous vote to be adopted, the bill passes if and only if it receives a unanimous vote. Otherwise, the bill is passed if and only if it receives a number of yes votes sufficient for adoption of whichever proposed rule-change within it requires the greatest number of yes votes.

If a bill is passed, all of its proposed rule-changes are adopted in the order in which they appear in the bill. If a bill is not passed, none of its proposed rule-changes are adopted.

  • Proposal #314 passed by 2/3 majority vote of 13–0
M.R. 315

One turn consists of three parts in this order:

  1. The moving player posts one bill in #player-rule-discussion for discussion and debate.
  2. The moving player proposes the final form of that bill in #voting and players cast their votes.
  3. Once voting ends, an admin adds the number of favourable votes received to the moving player's score, then posts the results of the turn in #rules-changelog along with any corresponding changes to mutable or immutable rules.

The moving player must propose their bill in #voting in the first 16 hours of a turn. If a bill is not proposed in said time, the turn is forfeit.

A turn begins when the results of the previous turn are posted in #rules-changelog.

  • Proposal #315 passed by 2/3 majority vote of 13–0
  • Amends M.R. 310
  • Amended by proposal #323, to become M.R. 323
M.R. 317

A proposed bill passes if and only if it receives a 2/3 majority of ""yes"" votes among all eligible voters, or, only if applicable to any of its proposals by the conditions of another rule, the number of ""yes"" votes needed to satisfy the conditions of that rule, if that would be a greater number of ""yes"" votes required.

If the time period allotted for voting on a proposed bill expires and the vote remains inconclusive, and the proposed bill is not eligible or no longer eligible for a time extension, then the first paragraph of this rule is void. The proposed bill will then be passed if and only if it receives a 3/4 majority of ""yes"" votes among valid votes cast, or, only if applicable to any of its proposals by the conditions of another rule, the number of ""yes"" votes among all valid votes cast needed to satisfy the conditions of that rule as if the valid votes cast represented the entire pool of eligible voters, if that would be a greater number of ""yes"" votes required.

  • Proposal #317 passed by 2/3 majority vote of 13–2
  • Amends M.R. 301
M.R. 318

Rule-changes that transmute immutable rules into mutable rules may be adopted if and only if they receive a unanimous ""yes"" among all eligible votes cast. Transmutation shall not be implied, but must be stated explicitly in a proposal to take effect.

M.R. 322

A new role will be created in the Nomic Discord server named ""Historian,"" and shall have these specific privileges as they pertain to the Discord server:

  • ""Send messages"" in #rules-changelog
  • ""Send messages"" in #resources

However, the Historian role shall have other, more symbolic privileges that, while not limited to the Discord server, shall remain enforced regardless:

  • Any new Historian must be given access to edit the ""NomicDOR"" Google Doc.
  • Any Historian that (willingly or unwillingly) loses the role in the Discord server must forfeit their editing privileges in this same Google Doc.

Those with the Historian role have these following occupational requirements:

  • Any rule-change with a conclusive vote or whose voting period has expired must be followed up with a log of the results and the effects of said vote in #rules-changelog. The conventions of these logs shall remain consistent with the ones as established as this game session began, and shall be posted as soon as possible following the conclusion of a rule-change vote.
  • The only exception to this requirement applies only if there are multiple users with the Historian role. If the vote following a proposal has concluded, and a different Historian has posted a log of the vote as defined in the previous section, then other Historians need not create additional logs. In other words, logs must be unique and shall not ever describe the same proposal.
  • Similarly, Historians are also responsible for scorekeeping, by using the Google Doc as mentioned prior. All previous conventions, including individual vote counting and tracking proposals/judge rulings, must be followed.

When this proposal passes, willarseny will be given the Historian role.

  • Proposal #322 passed by 2/3 majority vote of 12–1
